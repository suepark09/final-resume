React-Redux Battleship

Two player Battleship game with functional chat, built with React, Redux, Websockets, and Firebase.

About This Project And My Role

This was our final project for DigitalCrafts. We decided to recreate Battleship due to the game's ubiquity and simple gameplay. However, we wanted to take on the challenge of making it realtime, so that two players are able to play each other on different computers, while also being able to chat!


My role was to implement the action of selecting the different ships and placing it onto the board. This includes the logic for selecting a ship and being able to preview the placement by hovering over the board with the highlighted ship, while considering constraints such as preventing the user from spamming the same ship, overlapping ships, and placing ships outside the constraints of the board. My other tasks included redesigning and coding the app layout and UI.

Project Features

1. Select your ship and preview the placement by hovering over the board with the highlighted ship. Click on a square once you decided where you want to set the ship.

2. Click on the Ship Orientation button to rotate your ship either horizontally or vertically.

3. After placing the ships, click on the Start Game button to grab the link to send over to your opponent.

4. Enter your username to start chatting with your opponent!

5. Start attacking your opponent's ships. Squares that don't contain a ship are marked white.

4. Squares that contain a ship are marked red. You can also keep track of you and your opponent's hit-count by referring to the counters above the board.

5. Once either one of the player's ship count reaches zero, both are presented with either a win or lose modal.
